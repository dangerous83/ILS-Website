Why NVOCC

A faster, more flexible alternative to the mega-carriers

A Non-Vessel Operating Common Carrier (NVOCC) operates like a shipping line — issuing bills of lading and managing containers — but without owning the ships. Instead of being locked into one carrier's schedule and pricing, ILS draws on a wide network of vessel owners and shipping partners to route your sea freight the fastest, most cost-effective way available.

Faster

Direct vessel-owner connections

We book straight through vessel owners and partner lines, so space is secured and cargo sails sooner — without waiting in a single mega-carrier's queue.

Flexible

Setups built around your cargo

FCL, LCL, multi-supplier consolidation, switch B/Ls — we shape the booking to your shipment instead of forcing it into a rigid carrier product.

Competitive

Smarter routing & rates

With multiple carriers and lanes to choose from, we compare routings to hit the sweet spot between transit time and cost — not just one line's list price.

What's Included

Carrier capability, without the vessel

Our NVOCC operation gives shippers the control of a carrier and the flexibility of a forwarder — under one accountable bill of lading.

Equipment sourcing, positioning, and control across key lanes — so the right box is in the right port when you need it.

How It Works

From booking to release

A four-step routine — every container, every sailing, every time.

01

Booking

Tell us lane, volume, and equipment. We confirm space and issue a booking against our carrier contracts.

02

Documentation

We issue the House Bill of Lading and pre-file shipping instructions, so nothing waits at the terminal.

03

Sailing

Containers load and sail with live milestone tracking and one point of contact end-to-end.

04

Release

Arrival notice, customs, and cargo release coordinated for a clean, on-time handover.
